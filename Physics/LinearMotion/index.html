Linear Motion

Motion in a Straight Line

Kinematics equations for constant acceleration:

$$ v = u + at $$ $$ s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2 $$ $$ v^2 = u^2 + 2as $$

Where:
$u$ = initial velocity, $v$ = final velocity,
$a$ = acceleration, $t$ = time, $s$ = displacement.

Controls

Time (t): 0.00 s
Pos (s): 0.00 m
Vel (v): 0.00 m/s
Note: Observe how acceleration changes the rate at which the block's velocity increases. If acceleration is negative relative to velocity, it acts as retardation (braking).

Vector Addition

Resultant of two vectors $\vec{A}$ and $\vec{B}$:

$$ \vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B} $$ $$ |\vec{R}| = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + 2AB\cos\theta} $$

Drag the tips of Vector A and Vector B on the grid to see their resultant R.

Vector A: 0i + 0j
Vector B: 0i + 0j
Resultant R: 0i + 0j
Magnitude |R|: 0.00
Angle ($\theta$ from x-axis): 0.00°

Projectile Motion

Formulas for a projectile fired at angle $\theta$ with velocity $u$:

Time of Flight: $$ T = \frac{2u \sin\theta}{g} $$
Max Height: $$ H = \frac{u^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g} $$
Range: $$ R = \frac{u^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} $$
